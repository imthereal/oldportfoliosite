Localizing Facebook for India

Content Strategy at Facebook
Project Overview
Facebook is experiencing major growth in India, so catering content to these users is a high priority. I was tasked with creating standards for an Indian-English (en_IN) locale.
My Contributions
I wrote content standards based on user research insights. Additionally, I designed multiple A/B tests to test these standards in product (including the one in this project).
Background & Research
Interestingly, 95% of Facebook's monthly active users in India use Facebook in American English or British English despite user interface content existing in Hindi, Telugu, Bengali, and other popular Indian languages.

After digging into user research, we found that:
1. Many people in India see Facebook as an international gateway to learn and practice English.
2. Some Indian users actually prefer reading and typing in English due to keyboard limitations.
3. It's a jarring user experience to switch users to different language interfaces, especially because users' device interfaces tend to be in English.

Additionally, user research found that English in India is quite different from the English language variations currently supported by Facebook—it combines British English and American English with localized spelling, phrases and terminology.
Content Standards
After conducting more research interviews with people from India and working closely with our India internationalization (i18n) expert, I came up with these content standards for an en_IN locale:
‍
- Include directive, action-oriented copy
- Use concise but descriptive phrasing
- Replace words with multiple meanings (i.e.: Keep can mean "to possess" or "to continue")
- Remove homophones or similar sounding words (i.e.: "except" and "accept")
- Remove idiomatic/colloquial language specific to American or British English
- Include Indian-English colloquial phrasing and terminology

Testing

I designed multiple A/B test to determine the quantitative performance of these standards when compared to existing English copy. My main A/B test was performed on the "profile wizard," a NUX flow that guides new users through updating their Facebook profile information. The content in this flow presented many opportunities to test each of the standards I created. Additionally, a NUX flow targets new users who have less exposure to terminology within the Facebook ecosystem, eliminating bias from existing mental models.

We gated this test to users with geolocations in India with their locale set to en_US (American English) or en_UK (British English).

Results

Initially, the team thought that en_IN would be useful to increase content literacy for lower-to-middle class Indian users. I personally believe that en_IN content has more potential to improve the user experience for middle-to-upper class Indian users who have a working knowledge of English. By tailoring phrasing to locales, Facebook can increase its CAU (Cares About Users) metric.
Quantitative Results
Analysis showed an increase in click-through for every screen, as well as a statistically significant increase in flow completion.
Product Improvements
This flow was shipped to all Android and iOS users in India with English language settings shortly after I left my internship.
