Fractional Shares

Content Strategy at Robinhood
Project Overview
Fractional Shares allow users to invest in pieces of whole shares, enabling people to buy stocks or ETFs that they might not otherwise be able to afford. We were the first major brokerage to release this feature.
My Contributions
I developed the content strategy for this feature, which entailed naming and positioning the product, writing product (UX) copy, strategizing announcement communications, and developing customer support materials.
Our team conducted a series of foundational research interviews and quickly learned that most people had never heard of fractional shares. This meant that I needed to write copy concise enough for mobile devices, yet informational enough to explain the product, and enticing enough to inspire conversion, which proved to be my biggest challenge throughout the development of this feature.

While my initial content ideation was aspirational and value-focused, our foundational research caused us to pivot towards functional, educational copy. Our team believed—and later user research interviews proved—that this was the right move, given the limited mental models on fractional shares.
‍
We then conducted more research interviews to determine what information was absolutely necessary to gaining a conceptual understanding of fractional shares, then ran an unbranded survey with various value props and explanations to validate our qualitative insights. We determined that it was necessary to drill in the idea of parts of a whole share to best explain the concept, and further, that investing with "as little as $1" was the most salient benefit of the feature.
User Research

Waitlist Flow

As tradition at Robinhood, we started by launching a waitlist for Fractional Shares. Over 200,000 people joined the waitlist on the first day alone, which was covered on the CNBC show Mad Money. Additionally, over 1 million people joined the waitlist within the first month of announcement.

In-App Flow

Our in-app flow had to be consistent with the rest of the Robinhood ecosystem, but we wanted the Fractional Shares experience to be a bit more lightweight due to the likelihood that inexperienced investors were more likely to use this product. After launching the product, we ran a usability survey and received a 4.6 out of 5 for ease of use.
